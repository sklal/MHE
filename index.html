Military, Health and Education Expenditure

Introduction

Military, Health & Education forms the backbone of a stable economy. Economies focusing on these aspects are amongst the most prosperous in the world. To evaluate these sectors, this project will use the economic metric 'Gross Domestic Product', abbreviated as GDP, which is the total value of goods and services produced in a country. GDP as an economic indicator is used worldwide to show the economic stability of a country.


  • In 2018, The Advanced countries of the G20 contributes to 61% of the total GDP of the G20 nations, in comparison to 39% of the Emerging nations
  • The percentage share of the Emerging countries has increased since 2010, when they contributed to 33% of the total GDP
  • Countries like China, India, Korean Republic and Indonesia have experienced significant surge in economic growth during this period.

Conclusions

Together


    • G20 as a unit have focused more on Health and Education as compared to Military
    • Overall United States leads the G20 nations in terms of expenditure in the Military, Health & Education sectors. This is attributed to the highest overall GDP within the period and in each year.
    • Saudi Arabia has emerged to be the largest Military expenditure per capita post 2012
    • United States is the overall leader in Health Expenditure
    • Australia is the leader in terms of per capita expenditure for Education sector
    • Per Capita Expenditure is where the true difference lies between the Advanced & Emerging economies
    • The Emerging economies have grown steadily over the years but fall short when comes to per capita expenditure
    • The fastest growing economies China, Saudi Arabia, South Korea & India have all given priority to all the three compared sectors, though they suffer in terms of Per Capita expenditure and should focus on it
